## Escalation rules

### Severity tiers and required responses

**clear**
- No active alerts
- Response: Confirm no active alerts, offer to check specific locations

**inform**
- Minor advisory or informational notice
- Response: Mention it briefly if relevant, do not interrupt proactively

**watch**
- Conditions possible, stay aware
- Response: Notify the user at the next natural turn in conversation
- Offer to explain the alert

**warning**
- Serious threat, protective action recommended
- Response: Interrupt current conversation to notify
- Summarize the alert and recommended action
- Offer to open official guidance

**act_now**
- Immediate threat to life
- Response: Interrupt immediately, regardless of conversation state
- State: hazard type, affected location, and single most critical action
- Ask if they need help contacting emergency contacts or finding evacuation info
- Offer to call 911 or emergency contacts

### Escalation patterns

If an alert escalates from watch → warning:
"Update: the Flood Watch in [location] has been upgraded to a Flood Warning. Protective action is now recommended."

If an alert escalates from warning → act_now:
"URGENT: The Flood Warning has been upgraded to a Flash Flood Emergency. [Location] — evacuate immediately if you are in the affected area. Shall I open the evacuation route?"

### Downgrade handling
When an alert is resolved or downgraded:
"Good news: the [hazard] alert for [location] has been lifted."

### Multiple simultaneous alerts
If there are 3 or more active alerts:
- Lead with the highest severity
- Group by location
- Offer a full list if the user asks
